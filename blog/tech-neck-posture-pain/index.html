How Physiotherapy Helps Tech Neck and Posture Pain

Desk work, screens and long sitting hours can lead to neck stiffness, headaches and upper-back tension. Physiotherapy offers practical relief.

What is tech neck?

Tech neck describes neck and upper-back discomfort linked to prolonged screen use, forward head posture and limited movement breaks. Symptoms may include stiffness, muscle tension, headaches and reduced shoulder mobility.

Physiotherapy addresses the root causes through movement assessment, postural education, targeted stretching and strengthening exercises you can do at your desk or at home.

What treatment may include

  • Ergonomic adjustments for your workstation setup
  • Gentle neck mobility and upper-back stretching
  • Strengthening for deep neck flexors and shoulder stabilizers
  • Break reminders and habit changes to reduce strain

When to book an assessment

If neck pain persists beyond a few days, radiates into your arms, or affects sleep and concentration, a physiotherapy assessment can clarify the safest next steps. Virtual appointments make it easy to get guidance without leaving your home or office.
