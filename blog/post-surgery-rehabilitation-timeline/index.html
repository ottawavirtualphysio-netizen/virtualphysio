Post-Surgery Rehabilitation Timeline: What Patients Should Expect

Recovery is usually phased, and each stage has different goals, exercises, and precautions. A structured plan helps patients rebuild strength safely and confidently.

Phase 1: Early recovery

In the first days and weeks after surgery, the main priorities are protecting the surgical site, reducing swelling, and beginning gentle movement. Your physiotherapist will guide you through safe positioning, range-of-motion work, and pain management strategies.

Phase 2: Early mobilization

As healing progresses, movement becomes more active. This stage usually focuses on restoring comfort, improving basic mobility, and gradually rebuilding strength without overloading the recovery tissue.

Phase 3: Strengthening and return to function

Later in recovery, the plan shifts toward improving endurance, movement quality, and functional strength. This is where patients often begin to return to daily routines, work tasks, or more active hobbies.

Phase 4: Long-term maintenance

Once the main recovery milestones are met, maintenance exercises and ongoing movement habits help prevent re-injury and support long-term independence.
